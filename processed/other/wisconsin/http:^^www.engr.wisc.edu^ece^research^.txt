Research Areas




A major activity of the Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering's graduate program is the advancement of knowledge through
scientific research.  Faculty members in teh department actively
conduct research at the forefront of their specialties.  Every
graduate student inteh department is given the opportunity to conduct
research under faculty guidance as part of their degree program.
The various research programs and other professional activities of our
faculty are described in the following pages.  This booklet is
organized by area of specialization with faculty, labs, and research
in each area found in its particular section.  A list of selected
publications is also available.  For more information you are urged to
correspond directly with individual faculty members.
Graduate studies are offered in all areas of electrical and computer
engineering, and most specializations can be pursued in depth.  These
studies are presently organized into the following areas:


Power Engineering



Machines


Power Electronics


Power Systems



Computer Engineering



Architecture


Computer-Aided Design and Test



Electrophysics


EM Waves

Solid State and Microelectronics

Photonics

Plasmas



Systems


Control

Communication

Signal Processing

Biomedical
